About me

I am a researcher in ecology and evolution, interested in understanding how ecological and evolutionary processes interact to shape biodiversity patterns across spatial scales. My work integrates community ecology, macroecology, and evolutionary biology to investigate how environmental gradients, regional species pools, and biotic interactions could structure communities and drive trait divergence.

Recently, I studied Neotropical butterfly communities along replicated elevational gradients in distinct biogeographic regions. I also investigated the role of predator–prey interactions and chemical defenses in aposematic systems, combining ecological analyses, metabolomics, and behavioral experiments. More broadly, I use a combination of large biodiversity datasets, species distribution modelling, and phylogenetic approaches (mainly in R) to explore the ecological and evolutionary drivers of biodiversity and species coexistence.
